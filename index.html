The gap is not tools or talent — it's decision-ready insight.

Every funding cycle, the intelligence that informed it — what worked, what didn't, what the field actually needs — is lost. Evaluations rely on anecdote. New programmes start from what can be remembered, not what was learned.

Europe's cultural and creative industries generate approximately €477 billion annually.1 Ninety-nine percent of actors are small or micro-enterprises, creating primarily intangible value. The institutions that fund, govern, and shape this ecosystem make consequential decisions about creative value with inadequate intelligence.

The creative sector has historically lacked a shared system of record. Valuable insights remain trapped within individual funding cycles and fragmented reports. Moving from episodic evaluation to cumulative, decision-grade intelligence is not incremental — it is a structural shift.

Capital is misallocated because the value it funds cannot be measured.
