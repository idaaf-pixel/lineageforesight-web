Who this is for

Lineage Foresight works with institutions that shape Europe's creative economy

Organisations that commission creative work

You need to assess quality, predict sustainability, and justify investment in projects with qualitative, long-term value. Traditional metrics miss what matters.

Policymakers, funders, and investors

You allocate capital at scale but lack shared frameworks to compare outcomes across contexts or learn from past decisions. Each decision starts from scratch.

Creative practitioners and intermediaries

You produce deep cultural value through collaboration and practice, but lack tools to document, communicate, or demonstrate this value to funders and institutions.

Two ways to work with us:

Pilot partnerships

Build decision-ready intelligence inside real decision contexts

We work with a small number of institutional partners to build decision-ready intelligence inside real decision contexts. Not a report delivered from outside — a structured engagement where your knowledge of what matters meets our method for making it legible.

What makes this different from consultancy is what it produces. A pilot doesn't just generate insight for your organisation. It generates evidence for how decisions about creative value can be made differently — across institutions, across borders, over time. Your case becomes part of a growing infrastructure that makes the next decision, in the next organisation, better informed.

This is a founding commitment. We invest methodology and analytical capacity. You invest institutional context and real decisions. Both sides shape something that didn't exist before.

A limited number of pilot partnerships are available in 2026.

Research partnerships

Help build the first European baseline for decision-readiness

The first European baseline for decision-readiness in the creative economy is being built right now. Every assessment response contributes to it. Research partners go further.

A research partnership is a longer commitment to building the infrastructure itself — contributing sustained institutional perspective to an evidence base that compounds with every engagement. You don't receive a deliverable. You become part of how this field develops its capacity to know what it's doing.

A small number of founding research partnerships are open. This is a mutual investment — not a grant, not a fee for service, but a commitment to building something the sector has never had.

If you think the creative economy deserves better intelligence than it currently has, this is how it gets built.
